# Autonomy

## Definition

Autonomy is the degree to which an agent system can choose and carry out actions
without step-by-step human approval.

## Why It Matters

Autonomy changes risk. The more an agent can do on its own, the more important
permissions, logging, rollback, evaluation, and human control become.

## Example

An agent that drafts an email has low autonomy. An agent that sends the email to
customers without review has much higher autonomy.

## Common Confusions

- Autonomy is not one switch; it comes in levels.
- A system can be autonomous in one domain and tightly supervised in another.
- Autonomy is not the same as intelligence or trustworthiness.
