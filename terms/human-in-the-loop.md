# Human-in-the-Loop

## Definition

Human-in-the-loop means a person is intentionally involved in part of an AI
system's workflow, often to approve, review, correct, or decide.

## Why It Matters

Human review can lower risk and preserve agency, but it only works when the
human receives enough context to make a real decision.

## Example

An agent prepares a pull request summary, but a developer reviews the diff and
chooses whether to merge it.

## Common Confusions

- Human-in-the-loop is not useful if the human is only rubber-stamping opaque
  output.
- Adding a human step does not automatically make a workflow safe.
- The human's workload and attention are part of the system design.
